# Bear UP_TRI v3 — Final Production Review

## 1. Sub-regime gating rules: defensibility

**Strongest argument FOR**: The tri-modal structure directly encodes market regime with measurable, actionable thresholds. High vol + recent drawdown (hot) captures the exact environment where mean-reversion patterns have historically worked—panic selling creates technical oversold bounces. The warm zone cleverly isolates the "grinding bear" state (high vol but not yet deeply sold off) which your n=42 sample suggests may be even better. Cold regime forces you to wait for the highest-conviction Tier 1 setups when conditions are mixed. This is regime-aware execution, not just pattern matching.

**Strongest argument AGAINST**: Hard thresholds at vol=0.70 and 60d_return=-0.10 create artificial regime boundaries in continuous market conditions. A stock at vol=0.69 vs 0.71 shouldn't trigger completely different position sizing. The warm zone (95.2% WR on n=42) is dangerously under-sampled—42 trades is nowhere near statistical significance for a 95% claim, and you're building production logic around it. This risks overfitting to a lucky window. The regime definitions also don't account for *why* vol is high—a sharp crash (good for reversal) looks identical to elevated chop (bad for reversal) in this metric.

**The real trade-off**: You're choosing simplicity and executability (hard rules, clear gates) over adaptive precision. This works if the regime buckets are robust to small perturbations and if the warm zone actually exists as a stable phenomenon. If warm is just statistical noise, you'll discover that painfully in production when the 95.2% becomes 60%.

**What I'd probe**: Run a sensitivity analysis—how much do win rates change at vol=0.60/0.75/0.80 and returns=-0.05/-0.15? If the edges are smooth, you're capturing real regime structure. If WR drops off a cliff at 0.69 vs 0.71, you're curve-fitting.

## 2. WR calibration honesty

**The 94.6% → 70% haircut is appropriate but the reasoning needs scrutiny**. Your live performance almost certainly benefited from: (a) cherry-picking the strongest signals during ideal conditions, (b) human override/intuition that won't scale, (c) small sample luck, and (d) unintentional selection bias where marginal setups were skipped. Discounting by 24.6pp is a serious, conservative adjustment that suggests you understand these biases. The 65-75% band for hot regime also appropriately acknowledges within-regime variance.

**However, the warm zone calibration (70-90%) is dishonest**. You cannot claim 70-90% expected WR based on n=42 at 95.2% observed. The confidence interval on 40/42 wins is roughly 84-99% at 95% confidence—your lower bound of 70% implies you expect 20-25pp of regression, which contradicts the tight observed CI. Either the warm zone is real and should expect 80-95%, or it's noise and should inherit hot's 65-75%. You can't have both the high observed rate AND the skeptical calibration. Pick one: believe the data (and risk pain) or distrust it (and collapse warm into hot).

**What would change my view**: (1) Out-of-sample warm validation—find 40+ more warm regime trades from historical data outside your training window. If WR holds >85%, the zone is real. (2) Mechanistic explanation—why would warm (vol high, modest drawdown) outperform hot (vol high, severe drawdown)? If you can't explain it, it's likely spurious. (3) Phase-5 overlap—if warm trades are disproportionately Phase-5 validated, the 95% might be detector-driven, not regime-driven.

**Calibration gap**: You don't specify expected WR for *warm no Tier 1* since your rules say hot/warm both take full signals. If warm takes non-Tier-1 setups at full size expecting 70-90% but they actually run 55%, you'll bleed capital fast.

## 3. Edge cases that will break in production

**Edge Case 1: Regime whipsaw on boundary conditions**. SPY sits at vol=0.71, fires a Bear UP_TRI signal (hot regime, TAKE_FULL). Next day vol drops to 0.69 before exit—now cold regime. If a second signal fires same name, your repeat-name cap triggers TAKE_SMALL, but the position sizing decision was made under hot regime assumptions. Then vol spikes back to 0.72. Your sub-regime is flickering on 2-3% vol changes but your positions are locked in. This creates incoherent position books where sizing/stops don't match current regime. **Fix**: Add regime stability filter (require 3+ days in regime before switching, or use 5-day rolling avg for vol).

**Edge Case 2: Phase-5 override in cold regime with mismatch rule conflict**. Phase-5 detector validates a Health sector Bear UP_TRI signal in cold regime. Per hierarchy, "Phase-5 validated signals execute regardless of sub-regime." But your mismatch rules say "AVOID Health UP_TRI in Bear (32.4% lifetime)." Which wins—Phase-5 override or sector mismatch? Your doc says Phase-5 outputs are "unconditional priors" but mismatch rules are "universal." This will cause a production halt when trader has to make a judgment call in real-time. **Fix**: Explicit hierarchy: Phase-5 > mismatch rules > sub-regime. Or: Phase-5 signals in Health must show *additional* confirming factor (e.g., only if hot regime too).

**Edge Case 3: Correlation cap stacking with repeat-name cap**. Three Bear UP_TRI signals fire on same day (triggers day-correlation 80% sizing cap). One of them is a repeat name from 5 days ago (triggers repeat-name TAKE_SMALL cap). Does the trader take: (a) 80% of SMALL = 0.8 × 0.5 = 40% size, (b) just SMALL (50%), or (c) just 80%? Your rules don't specify cap precedence or whether they stack. If they stack multiplicatively, a hot regime repeat-name same-day signal becomes 0.8 × 0.5 = 40% of base, which might be below your minimum position threshold, forcing a SKIP. **Fix**: Explicit stacking rule: "Apply most conservative cap, do not stack" or "Stack multiplicatively with 25% floor."

**Edge Case 4: December mismatch during hot regime with Phase-5 validation**. December + hot regime + Phase-5 validated signal. December rule says AVOID (-25pp lifetime). Hot regime says TAKE_FULL. Phase-5 says execute unconditionally. If you skip, you violate Phase-5 override. If you take, you violate seasonality mismatch. **Fix**: "December mismatch applies even to Phase-5 unless hot regime + Tier 1 confluence" or quantify the interaction—maybe December hot is only -10pp, not -25pp.

**Edge Case 5: Max position limit (5 concurrent) reached when Phase-5 + hot regime signal arrives**. You're holding 5 Bear UP_TRI positions. A Phase-5 validated hot regime Tier 1 signal fires—your highest conviction possible setup. Do you: (a) skip it (violates Phase-5 hierarchy), (b) close weakest existing position (but what if it's in profit/about to exit?), or (c) override the 5-position cap? **Fix**: "Phase-5 in hot overrides position cap; max becomes 6 with forced closure of oldest position within 24hrs."

## 4. Trader emotional preparation: what they'll actually feel

**The math (28W/2L → 21W/9L) understates the psychological shock**. A trader running at 93-95% for weeks develops a *mental model* where losses are anomalies—flukes, missed signals, bad fills. When loss #3 hits in a 10-trade span (now 70% WR), the emotional narrative shifts from "I made a mistake" to "the system is broken." This triggers crisis mindset: second-guessing entries, manually overriding signals, checking if the detector is malfunctioning, and worst of all, size reduction out of fear right before the win streak resumes. The trader won't feel 70% WR—they'll feel *failure 30% of the time*, which is viscerally different from the intellectual understanding of a 70% backtest.

**Concrete behavioral dynamics**: After 2 losses in 4 trades (50% locally vs 70% expected), the trader will face signal #5 with dread, not confidence. They'll look for reasons to skip—"vol is at 0.71, basically cold," "this signal looks weaker," "maybe I should wait for Phase-5." If they take it and lose again (3L in 5, now 40%), they'll reduce size on signal #6 to "feel it out," which guarantees they'll miss the edge when the win streak resumes. By trade 12-15, if WR is tracking 70% (9W/6L), the trader may lobby to pause deployment, claiming "production doesn't match backtest" even though 70% *was* the calibrated expectation.

**What actually helps**: (1) **Daily journaling with WR tracking**: Trader logs expected WR per trade (hot=70%, warm=80%, cold+Tier1=60%) and cumulative production WR. Seeing "I'm at 68% after 25 trades, right on target" defuses panic. (2) **Pre-mortem on 3-loss streaks**: Before Wave 5, trader writes down *in advance* what they'll do when 3 losses hit in 5 trades—"I will take signal #6 at full size per regime rules, I will not review backtest, I will not message team claiming breakage." Having a pre-committed response breaks the crisis loop. (3) **Separate P&L from WR tracking**: Trader tracks WR% and average R-multiple separately. Three small losses + two big wins can feel like failure (60% WR) but show profit (+1.2R net). Reframe "wins" as R-multiple, not binary outcomes.

**The real enemy is mid-streak regime doubt**: After 15 trades at 70% WR (10W/5L), if the next 5 go 5W/0L (now 75% cumulative, perfectly on target), the trader will feel *vindicated* that "it's working again" and might over-size, setting up for pain when regression hits. Emotional volatility lags statistical reality by 5-10 trades. **Mitigation**: Fixed position sizing based on regime only, zero discretion. Remove the valve the trader will instinctively turn.

Does a hot regime trade taken at vol=0.75 use the same stop as a cold+Tier1 trade? If hot regime trades mean-revert faster (2-day exits) vs cold+Tier1 needing 5-day holds, your WR expectations are wrong because you're mixing different hold-time distributions.

**Missing #2: Signal strength/quality tiering within regime**. Your rules treat all hot regime signals identically (TAKE_FULL with caps), but surely a hot+Phase-5+Tier1 signal is stronger than hot+non-Tier1+no-Phase-5. You have the components (Phase-5, Tier 1) but no combinatorial matrix. If (a) is 85% WR and (d) is 60%, treating them the same will cause the 65-75% hot expectation to drift unpredictably based on signal mix. **Why critical**: When the trader sees a "weak" hot signal (no Phase-5, no Tier 1), they need permission to either TAKE_SMALL or know it's still worth full size. Right now, the rules force uniform treatment, which invites manual override.

**Missing #3: Performance monitoring triggers and pause conditions**. At what point do you halt deployment to review? If hot regime WR drops to 55% over 30 trades (vs 65-75% expected), is that statistical noise or system degradation? You've prepared the trader for 3-loss streaks (2.7% probability), but what about 10W/10L over 20 trades (50% WR in hot regime)? That's a ~5% probability event at true 70% WR—unlikely but not impossible. Without pre-defined "stop and audit" triggers, the team will argue in real-time about whether to continue, with recency bias dominating. **Specification needed**: "If hot regime WR falls below 60% over 40+ trades (2+ stdev below 70%), pause new entries and audit signal quality. If cold+Tier1 drops below 50% over 30 trades, reduce sizing to TAKE_SMALL pending review."

**Under-specified bonus: Warm regime boundary ambiguity**. Warm is defined as "vol > 0.70 AND -0.10 ≤ 60d_return < 0" but what happens at 60d_return = -0.0001? Technically warm, but economically identical to cold (60d_return = +0.0001). If warm has vastly different WR expectations (70-90% vs cold 58-65%), you're making huge execution decisions on sub-1% return differences. Add dead zone: "If -0.02 < 60d_return < 0, classify as cold for conservative execution."
